waterfrontline

The water’s edge is the front line.


The first US casualties of climate change are the small towns, farms, and rural workers along its flooding inland rivers.


Look: if climate change were a real thing, you’d see crazy things all over the country, not on the coasts.


You’d see things like a mile-long, 20-ft tall iceberg landing on a farm in Nebraska after demolishing a dam.

Or flash floods, landslides, and rockslides across Eastern Kentucky.

Or a small town becoming an island in Black Earth, Wisconsin, with the bridge disappearing from beneath your feet.

Or Deweyville, Texas flooding yearly, even without getting rain. Plus, there would be floating caskets.

And New York - you’d see Lake Ontario flooding Henderson, Sodus, & Ogdensburg, and threatening Montreal.

And Missouri. There would be towns that watched their rehab efforts get flooded multiple times a year.

And animals. You’d see animals being lost due to weather.


You’d see tamarack forests getting destroyed in Minnesota because their predator, the eastern larch beetle, no longer has

to withstand a frozen winter and can eat all year long. So it does.

You’d see 500-year floods every year in Texas, leading to a redefinition of the term - and rezoning thousands of existing 

homes into that floodplain while bridges get swept away. 


You’d see farmers losing farms faster than ever, and then leading the discussion on farming, suicides, and help. You matter.


Look:

That’s what you’d see.

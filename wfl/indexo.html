waterfrontline

The water’s edge is the front line.


The first US casualties of climate change are the small towns, farms, and rural workers along its flooding inland rivers.


Look: If climate change were a real thing, we’d see crazy things all over the country, not on the coasts.


We’d see things like a mile-long, 20-ft long iceberg landing on a farm in Nebraska after demolishing a dam.

Or wave after wave of torrents, landslides, and rockslides all across Eastern Kentucky.

Or a small town becoming an island in Black Earth, Wisconsin, with the bridge disappearing from beneath your feet.

Or a village in Missouri suffering 3 major floods in a year as they try to rebuild in the flood plain after the climate changed.

Or Deweyville, Texas flooding out 3 straight years, sometimes even without getting rain. Plus, floating caskets.

And Lake Ontario flooding Henderson, Sodus, & Ogdensburg, New York, plus Montreal.


We’d see tamarack forests getting destroyed in Minnesota because their predator, the eastern larch beetle, no longer has

to withstand a frozen winter and can eat it all year long. So it does.

We’d see 100-year storms every year in Texas, leading to a redefinition of the term - and rezoning thousands of existing 

homes into that floodplain while bridges get swept away. 


We’d see farmers losing farms faster than ever, and then leading the discussion on farming, suicides, and help. You matter.


Look:

The waterline will rise higher. The battle lines will move higher. To see what happens as water rises, pick a town.

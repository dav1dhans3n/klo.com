The water’s edge is the front line.


The first US casualties of climate change are the small towns, farms, and rural workers along its flooding inland rivers.

 These are their stories, before and after they were thrust into the front lines.

Black Earth, Wisconsin. When a small town becomes a temporary island. 

Lewis and Clark Village, Missouri. Life in the flood plain after the climate changed.

Eastern Kentucky. The vicious cycle of torrents, landslides, and rockslides.

Deweyville, Texas. When you flood without getting rain. Plus, floating caskets.

Henderson, Sodus, & Ogdensburg, New York, plus Montreal: The flood in slow motion.

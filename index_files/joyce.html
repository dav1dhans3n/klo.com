A note regarding Joyce’s care:

While at BayView, and particularly while her heart medications were getting stabilized, Joyce was in intensive care. 

When her meds were stabilized and her breathing tube was removed so she could speak, her care level was lowered

but she remained in the ICU area to maintain a consistency of medical care and limit opportunities for infection. The 

number of visits from nursing staff members dropped but they were always right outside her door, literally.


At Genesis, the ratio of staff to patients is significantly lower than it was in the ICU. She is being visited regularly 

during rounds (during one two-hour period we counted 7 different staffers coming through) but on the weekends the 

staffing is noticeably lighter. If you are visiting and you see that she has unmet needs due to the staff/patient ratio, 

please intervene on her behalf. The nursing station for hall B is right down the hall from her but in her condition she 

can neither reach it physically or be heard there. For a while we will be her legs and voice.


Additionally, if you become aware of medical issues you feel are not being addressed, please let the medical staff know.
